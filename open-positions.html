Vision Upgrade Pioneer - Transhuman color perception

"To boldly know What Mary Didn't Know"

In the vulgar conception, color is the experience of particular frequencies of light. In reality we have known since Newton that there is no color in the light. It is not possible to produce all the colors we experience using individual frequencies of light, and we rarely encounter a single frequency of light on its own except when dealing with lasers. The colors we see result from mapping mixtures of light to a roughly three dimensional space that is a fabrication of our brains and has no basis in the external world. There is no reason other than ignorance and the failure of our imaginations to suspect that the colors we experience are all that are possible.

Since 2010 we have had clear proof of the existence of human tetrachromats. This occurs naturally only in women and is rare, but through psychophysical modeling it has been shown that some of these women perceive an additional independent dimension of color. Since they were not previously "normal" trichromats they cannot compare their experience to ours well but we do find them using multiple color terms to describe their visual experience of a single point in space.

In 2012 it was shown that it is not necessary for additional inputs to be present during early childhood development; with gene therapy it is possible to expand the color perception of an adult primate.

Using a method dating back to 1855 with the work of James Clerk Maxwell, in 2014 our team was able to non-invasively induce a novel color experience in an adult human. This subject reported seeing a color that was between red and orange but which was not reddish orange.

Based on our models it should be possible to use this method to bring about human pentachromacy resulting in color experiences beyond those experienced by any human before.

We are currently seeking a Python programmer for paired programming work to re-validate our simulations prior to the next experiment.
